## 2. NFT ART PLATFORMS — LANDSCAPE ANALYSIS

### SuperRare

**Current State (2025-2026):**
- Premier curated NFT art marketplace on Ethereum
- Positioned as the "gallery" of the NFT world — high curation standards
- Has its own governance token: $RARE

**Application Process:**
- Artists must submit an application form and be selected
- Highly competitive — acceptance rate is low
- Application asks for portfolio, social media presence, artistic statement
- **Pro tip from community:** Engage with SuperRare curators on Twitter; build community and visibility before applying
- Having existing collectors and a track record helps significantly
- Recent reports (2025) suggest the verification process may have evolved — some confusion about finding the "Verify as Artist" URL

**Fee Structure:**
- **Primary sales:** 15% commission to SuperRare, 85% to artist
- **Secondary sales:** 10% royalty to original creator
- **Collector royalties:** First collector receives 1% transaction royalty (taken from network fee)
- Smart contract-enforced royalties — guaranteed, not optional

**Generative/Code-Based Art:**
- SuperRare is primarily known for 1/1 artworks and editions, not long-form generative series
- Code-based art is welcome but the platform is more suited to individual pieces or small editions
- Not the ideal platform for on-chain generative art (that's Art Blocks/fxhash territory)

**Best For:** Established artists selling 1/1 digital pieces at premium prices. The "blue chip gallery" of NFT art.

### Art Blocks / Bright Moments

**Current State (2025-2026):**
- **Art Blocks 500 milestone:** Approaching 500 flagship projects published by November 2025
- Final Art Blocks Curated project: **"Quine" by Larva Labs** (creators of CryptoPunks) — symbolic closing of the curated collection
- Has matured from "collectibles" to recognized contemporary art platform
- Museum of the Moving Image exhibition: "Compositions in Code: The Art of Processing and p5.js" (through August 2025)

**How It Works:**
- Artists write generative algorithms in JavaScript (often p5.js)
- Code is stored on-chain on Ethereum
- Each mint generates a unique output from the algorithm using a blockchain hash as seed
- Collectors don't choose their output — it's the "surprise" element

**Tiers (Historical):**
- **Curated:** Highest tier, juried by Art Blocks curation board (now closing at 500)
- **Presents:** Current flagship tier for selected projects
- **Factory/Engine:** More open, self-service tier for artists

**Curation Process:**
- Very selective for flagship projects
- Artists typically need existing recognition in generative art community
- Technical quality of the algorithm is paramount
- Aesthetic consistency across outputs matters (every output should be "good")
- Art Blocks team reviews and may reach out to artists they discover

**Bright Moments:**
- Sister project/community focused on IRL minting experiences
- Hosts events where generative art is minted in person
- Bridges digital and physical art worlds

**Best For:** Serious generative artists creating long-form algorithmic series. The gold standard for on-chain generative art.

### fxhash

**Current State (2025-2026):**
- **fxhash 2.0** launched with Ethereum integration (originally Tezos-only)
- Now **multi-chain:** Tezos + Ethereum + Base network
- Most accessible platform for generative artists — open, permissionless
- Active development and innovation continuing

**Key Features:**
- **fx(params):** Allows collectors to interact with parameters during minting — they can influence the output within artist-defined constraints
- **Art Coins (FXH Protocol):** Launched on Base network. Artists can create ERC-20 tokens (1M supply) linked to their collections via bonding curves. Enables investment-like dynamics for art collections
- **$FXH Token:** Platform governance/utility token. Unlocked for trading on exchanges. Aims to create art coin indexes (similar to what Docker does for Zora)
- **Open-form generative art:** No curation gatekeeping — any artist can publish
- **Redeemables:** Physical/digital redemption tied to NFTs

**Q3 2025 Report highlights:**
- Working on index-level investments for art coins
- In discussions with entities creating indexes of top art coins
- Continued platform growth

**Best For:** Emerging generative artists who want to start minting immediately. Interactive generative art. Building an audience and experimenting with new economic models (art coins). Lower barrier to entry than Art Blocks.

## 3. JAVASCRIPT WEB PROGRAMMING FOR GENERATIVE ART

### Key Technologies

| Technology | Purpose | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **p5.js** | 2D creative coding | Gold standard for generative art. p5.js 2.0 launched with Tezos Foundation educational partnership. Used on Art Blocks extensively. Processing Foundation + Museum of the Moving Image exhibition (2025) |
| **Three.js** | 3D web graphics | Increasingly used for generative art (see Codrops tutorial Jan 2025). Good for 3D generative works on Art Blocks |
| **WebGL/GLSL** | GPU shaders | Maximum performance for complex visual effects. Used directly or through Three.js. Essential for particle systems and real-time effects |
| **Canvas API** | Low-level 2D drawing | Foundation beneath p5.js. Direct use for performance-critical work |
| **Web Audio API** | Audio synthesis/analysis | Enables audio-reactive art. Built into browsers. Analyze frequencies for visual mapping |
| **Tone.js** | Web audio synthesis | Higher-level wrapper for Web Audio API. Great for generative music + visual art combos |

**p5.js vs Three.js for Generative Art (per Transient Labs, Oct 2025):**
- p5.js: ideal for 2D work, waveform/bar audio visualization
- Three.js: better for 3D, complex lighting, immersive environments
- Both work for audio-reactive pieces

### Novel Approaches & Interesting Algorithms

**Classic Techniques (Still Relevant):**
- **Flow fields** — Vector fields driving particle movement (Tyler Hobbs' Fidenza made this iconic)
- **Particle systems** — Emergent behavior from simple rules
- **Cellular automata** — Conway's Game of Life variants, rule-based emergence
- **L-systems** — Recursive growth patterns, botanical simulation
- **Fractal noise (Perlin/Simplex)** — Foundation of organic-feeling generative art

**Cutting Edge (2025-2026):**
- **AI-assisted generative art:** Diffusion model outputs as inputs to code-based systems (hybrid approaches). NOT pure AI generation — the interesting work uses AI as one tool alongside hand-written algorithms
- **Interactive/real-time:** fx(params) on fxhash lets collectors influence output. Browser-based interactivity (Bjørn Staal's "Entangled")
- **Audio-reactive web art:** Web Audio API analyzing real-time audio input to drive visual parameters. Tone.js for generative music that drives generative visuals
- **Synthetic biology simulations:** Algorithms mimicking organic growth, recursion, fluidity (Bjørn Staal's "Immaterial")
- **Psychogeographic mapping:** Data-driven cartography as art (Staal's "Losing Oneself")
- **On-chain complexity:** Pushing the limits of what can be computed from a single hash seed
- **Multi-sensory browser experiences:** Combining visual + audio + interaction in single web-based pieces

## 4. STRATEGY BRIEF

### Recommended Path for an Emerging Artist with VP/Creative Tech Credentials

**Your Unique Positioning:**
Virtual production expertise is a *significant differentiator* in the generative art world. Very few generative artists come from VP — most come from graphic design, computer science, or fine art. VP gives you:
- Understanding of real-time rendering pipelines
- 3D spatial thinking
- Lighting and camera knowledge
- Experience with complex technical systems
- Understanding of how digital environments feel "real"

**Bjørn Staal is your closest precedent.** He went from running a design studio (Void) that did architectural installations and interactive environments → to full-time blockchain generative artist. His path took ~2 years from first Art Blocks experiment to full-time artist.

### Platform Recommendation

**Start: fxhash** (immediate, no gatekeeping)
- Publish your first generative works immediately
- Use fx(params) to create interactive pieces — this shows technical depth
- Build an audience and collector base
- Experiment with art coins for economic engagement
- Multi-chain support means broader reach

**Goal: Art Blocks** (medium-term, 6-18 months)
- After building a body of work and community on fxhash
- Art Blocks flagship (Presents) is the most prestigious generative art context
- Your VP background + proven fxhash track record would be compelling for curation
- This is where the Bjørn Staal trajectory points

**Parallel: SuperRare** (for 1/1 pieces)
- If you create standout individual works (not long-form series), SuperRare is the premium 1/1 market
- Apply after you have some blockchain art track record
- Good for pieces that bridge VP aesthetics with generative processes
