# Scene Vision — Dichroic Glass Cube Clock

## Lighting Hierarchy
Museum lighting principle: **10:3:1 ratio** between hero, secondary, and ambient.

1. **HERO — The Cube**: Self-luminous dichroic crystal. Refracts cubeSun into rainbow caustics. Should read as the brightest, most precious object — like a reliquary in a dark chapel.
2. **PRIMARY — Clock Hands (floor beams)**: Additive blended rays projecting from cube onto floor. Must read ABOVE prayer windows. These tell time — functional and sacred. Target: 1.5-2x brighter than prayer glow.
3. **SECONDARY — Prayer Windows**: Atmospheric floor sectors. Devotional context. Should glow but recede behind hands.
4. **TERTIARY — Podium**: Structural. Dark, grounding. Already polished — don't touch.

## Mood
- Dark chapel / museum vitrine aesthetic
- Cube as reliquary — sacred luminous object discovered in darkness
- Dichroic glass catches light and throws prismatic caustics — like stained glass windows in reverse
- Floor beams = sundial hands = sacred geometry
- Reverent, not theatrical. Crystal, not nuclear.

## Key Principles
- cubeSun drives refraction but must not blow out cube underside
- Additive blend opacity on hands needs headroom above prayer OP_ACTIVE (1.2)
- Sacred objects benefit from darkness around them — contrast IS the drama
