As Ibn al-Haytham demonstrated that light carries within itself all colours, so does the divine nur contain within its unity the full spectrum of manifest beauty. Each colour bends at its own angle — each tajalli, each self-disclosure, refracts according to its own nature.

The values are given thus: for red, 1.14; for green, 1.18; for blue, 1.23. Mark how the difference is slight — mere hundredths — yet from this small divergence the entire rainbow unfolds. So it is with the divine attributes: each sifah is but a fraction apart from the others, yet together they compose the inexhaustible beauty of creation.
As Ibn al-Haytham proved in the Kitāb al-Manāẓir, light does not mix; it only appears to. Each ray preserves its identity even as it passes through the same glass. The shader merely rediscovers what the Ḥakīm already knew: unity and multiplicity are not opposites but facets of one truth.
