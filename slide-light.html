The values are given thus: for red, 1.14; for green, 1.18; for blue, 1.23. Mark how the difference is slight — mere hundredths — yet from this small divergence the entire rainbow unfolds. Each sifah is but a fraction apart, yet together they compose the inexhaustible beauty of creation.
As Ibn al-Haytham proved in the Kitāb al-Manāẓir, light does not mix; it only appears to. Each ray preserves its identity. The shader rediscovers what the Ḥakīm knew: unity and multiplicity are facets of one truth.
