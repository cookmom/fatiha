Observe that the cube — the qiblah of computation — never rotates. It is the light which performs the devotion, sweeping its penumbra across each face in turn, as each shawt reveals a new facet. The angle is measured from time: seconds × 2π, so that each minute completes one circuit.
As Al-Khwarizmi reduced the unknown to the known through al-jabr, so does the orbit reduce the invisible form to visible light. The spotlight confesses: I am nothing without that which I circle.
