## I. THE PROPORTIONAL SYSTEM

### Ibn Muqla's Revolution (886–940 CE)

Abu Ali Muhammad ibn Ali ibn Muqla transformed calligraphy from artisanal craft into geometric science. His **al-khatt al-mansub** ("proportioned script") uses three measurements:

1. **Nuqta**: Rhombic dot from pressing nib flat — atomic measurement unit
2. **Alif**: Vertical stroke measured in nuqtas (5–9 depending on script)
3. **Circle**: Invisible circle, diameter = alif height. All curves derive from its arcs

### Alif Heights by Script
| Script | Dots | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Thuluth | 9 | Titles, architecture |
| Muhaqqaq | 9 | Large Qurans |
| Rayhani | 7 | Companion to Muhaqqaq |
| Tawqi' | 7 | Official correspondence |
| Naskh | 5 | Quran copying, printing |
| Riqa' | 5 | Administrative |

### Mathematical Properties (PLOS ONE)
- Width of alif = S × cos(α), where S = dot side, α = pen angle
- Edge Connection at 45°: P = 2n
- Historical dot counts varied: Al-Hiti (5/7/9), Al-Qalqashandi (6), Ikhwan al-Safa (8)

### Yaqut al-Musta'simi (d. 1298)
First to cut the nib at an **oblique angle** rather than straight. Became universal standard. Trained six specialist students. Completed ~1,001 Quran copies.

## II. THE QALAM (Reed Pen)

- Cut from reed between two nodes; **seasoned in horse manure for four years**
- Slit down center of nib (2–7mm) holds ~one letter's worth of ink
- Held between thumb, middle, and index finger; lightest possible pressure
- Writing through finger expansion, not arm movement
- **Ink**: soot (from mosque lamp interiors) + gum arabic + water. Stored in inkwell with raw silk fibers

### Pen Angles by Script
| Script | Angle | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Thuluth | 25–90° | **Variable** — changes within strokes |
| Naskh | 45° standard, 60° for alif | Mostly fixed |
| Diwani | 25–30° | Thin verticals, thick horizontals |
| Ruq'ah | Tilted lower right | Speed-optimized |
| Kufic | 45° | Traditional |

## III. SCRIPT PROFILES

### Thuluth (ثلث)
- "One-third" — 1/3 of each letter slopes
- **The mastery script**: "You are not a calligrapher until you can write Thuluth"
- 9-nuqta alif with 3-dot crochet at top
- **Variable pen angles** (the defining challenge): 70–75° descenders, 25–35° horizontals, 85–90° risers
- **Signature**: upward hooks on descending stroke terminals
- Letters frequently interlace; many alternate forms
- Paired with Naskh (Thuluth for titles, Naskh for body)

### Naskh (نسخ)
- "Copying" — efficient manuscript production
- 5-nuqta alif; mostly fixed 45° pen angle
- **Most common script worldwide**; basis for Arabic typefaces
- Equal flat/round shapes; balanced heavy/light strokes
- Full diacritical marks; highly legible
- 22 letters connect both sides; 6 right-only (alif, dal, dhal, ra, zay, waw)

### Diwani (ديوانى)
- Ottoman palace script; **a secret of the Sultan's palace**
- Created by Ibrahim Munif (15th c.) under Mehmed II
- ~45° slanted baseline; 25–30° pen angle
- **Undotted** (security feature); unconventional joins; no descenders (except meem)
- **Jeli Diwani**: maximum decoration, all spaces filled, sentences as single blocks

### Ruq'ah (رقعة)
- Standardized by Mumtaz Efendi (1810–1872)
- **Most common handwriting in Arab world today**
- Saved scribes ~2 seconds per word
- Short, straight strokes; minimal ornamentation; rarely needs diacritical marks

### Kufic (كوفي)
- Named after Al-Kufa, Iraq; emerged late 7th century
- **Angular, geometric** — straight lines and sharp angles
- Horizontal orientation; letters typically 2× wider than tall
- **Varieties**: Simple, Foliated, Floriated, Plaited/Knotted, Square/Geometric
- **Square Kufic**: Letters on grid cells (always odd rows/columns); white space = black space; no 2×2 contiguous blocks; words can rotate 90°. Originally Iranian brickwork (**banna'i** = masonry script)
- Notable: **Blue Quran** — gold Kufic on indigo vellum

## IV. TEZHIP (Illumination)

### Techniques
1. **Plain illumination**: Standard gold + color
2. **Halkari**: Flowers painted in dissolved gold washes
   - *Sulandirma*: Gold concentrating at petal tips, fading toward center — flowers illuminated from within
   - *Tarama*: Sweeping gold in delicate strokes
   - *Noktalama*: Stippling gold in dots
3. **Zerenderzer**: Gold-on-gold for dimensional pattern-within-pattern
4. **Zerefsan**: Gold sprinkling/scattering

### The Five Motifs
- **Rumi**: Stylized animal wings/talons → pure ornament (Seljuk origin)
- **Hatayi**: Stylized flowers from above/side (Chinese influence). Penç = 5-petal face-on rosette
- **Bulut**: Stylized Chinese clouds — water, abundance, rebirth
- **Cintemani**: Three dots + two wavy curves — leopard spots + tiger stripes = power/sovereignty
- **Munhani**: Serried curves (Seljuk)

### Key Masters
- **Baba Nakkas**: Topkapi atelier head under Mehmed II
- **Shah Kulu**: Iranian emigre, saz style pioneer under Suleiman
- **Kara Memi**: Naturalistic floral revolution (370 patterns in one text, c. 1566)

## V. TEXT-DECORATION RELATIONSHIP

### Hierarchy
Calligraphy (Word of God) > Illumination (Grace of God) > Illustration > Binding

**The text determines composition.** Tezhip exists to "dress the writing."

### Hilye-i Serif
Non-figurative "portrait" of the Prophet through calligraphic description. Layout named anthropomorphically (head/belly/crescent/belt/skirt/armpits). Central circle (*gobek*) contains physical description. Crescent (*hilal*) most elaborately decorated. Four Caliphs in corners. Established by Hafiz Osman (1691–92).

### Levha
Wall-display calligraphic panels in large Thuluth. Letters arranged to fill space artistically; reading deliberately difficult. Diacritics fill empty spaces. Gold frame with flowers.

## VI. MATERIALS & PIGMENTS

### Gold
- **Leaf gold**: Laid on adhesive (white lead + gum arabic), burnished with agate *muhre*
- **Shell gold**: Powdered, painted with brush. Multiple carats/shades available
- Burnishing creates mirror-like shine

### Lapis Lazuli
- Mined at **Sar-e-sang, Afghanistan**; status comparable to gold by 13th c.
- Grinding: granite → ceramic → polished alabaster pestle/mortar. Sulphur odor during grinding
- Simply crushing produces grey-blue (impurities); purification essential
- Tempered with gum arabic, honey, and milk

### Key Pigments
| Pigment | Color |
|---|---|
| Vermilion (HgS) | Bright red |
| Cochineal/kermes | Crimson |
| Orpiment (arsenic sulfide) | Yellow |
| Saffron | Yellow |
| Indigo | Blue |
| Malachite | Green |
| Verdigris | Green |
| Lampblack | Black |

### Paper (Ahar)
Coated with wheat starch + alum + egg white sizing. Burnished with agate until "one's reflection can be seen." Dyed with tea, onion skins, walnut skins, saffron, henna, indigo.

## VII. BOTANICAL SYMBOLISM

### The Four Flowers (Quatre Fleurs)
- **Tulip (Lale)**: Shares letters with Allah; divine/modesty; bows when bloomed
- **Rose (Gul)**: Represents Prophet Muhammad; Sufi love for Divine
- **Carnation (Karanfil)**: Life/renewal; sultans held carnations in portraits
- **Hyacinth (Sumbul)**: Beloved's curls; Rumi's "Exalted Hyacinth"

### Saz Style
"Enchanted forest" — oversized composite blossoms, feathery serrated leaves, mythical creatures (dragons, phoenixes). Peaked under Murad III (1574–1595).

### Garden = Paradise
*Pairidaeza* (Persian "enclosed by walls") → "paradise." Four gardens, four rivers (water, milk, honey, wine). Green = Quranic color of Paradise.

## VIII. TYPOGRAPHY — AUTHENTIC vs GENERIC

### Why Arabic Fonts Fail for Art
- Fixed 4-form model vs calligraphy's infinite contextual variations
- Arabic needs **12 typographic levels** (vs Latin's 5)
- Fonts are **lookup tables**; calligraphy is a **generative process**
- Kashida (elongation) reduced to flat underscore in digital; in calligraphy it's an art with subtle curvature

### DecoType / Thomas Milo
- ACE (Arabic Calligraphic Engine) stores **rules** not glyphs
- Generates Bezier curves dynamically; ~2,800 glyphs across 8 levels
- Won Dr. Peter Karow Award for Font Technology

### OpenType Feature Order for Arabic
GSUB: `ccmp` → `isol` → `fina` → `medi` → `init` → `rlig` → `rclt` → `calt`
GPOS: `curs` → `kern` → `mark` → `mkmk`

### Best Fonts
- **Diwan Mishafi**: 3,000+ forms, TDC Award (best professional)
- **Amiri**: Classical Naskh, Bulaq Press inspired (best free)
- **Scheherazade New**: Extended Arabic, full OpenType (SIL)

## XI. ZOOMORPHIC CALLIGRAPHY

Letters formed into animal shapes (primarily Ottoman Turkey, India, Qajar Iran from ~1458). Lions, peacocks, storks, deer. Thuluth, Naskh, Nasta'liq scripts used. Calligraphers elongate, wrap, rotate letters to create both contour and detail.

**Tughra compositions**: Ottoman sultan monograms evolved into zoomorphic forms — pigeons, falcons, peacocks, lions, horses. Also floral/botanical tughras.

## XII. COMPASS-AND-RULER CONSTRUCTION

### The Role of the Muhandis
Architecture was "practical geometry" in the early Islamic period. Every project involved a **muhandis** (مهندس, geometer). **Abu al-Wafa al-Buzjani** (940–998 CE) codified 100+ constructions in *Kitab fi ma yahtaj ilayh al-sani' min al-a'mal al-handasiyya* ("What the Craftsman Needs of Geometric Constructions"). Mathematicians taught artisans through cut-and-paste methods.

### The Circle as Origin
The circle is "the ultimate source of all diversity in creation." The radius fits exactly **6 times** around the circumference — foundation for all divisions. The divided circle never appears in the final figure — it is hidden scaffolding.

### Circle Division (Compass and Straightedge Only)

**6-fold** (foundation): Draw mother circle, place compass at left/right intersections, draw full circles → 6 equally spaced points (hexagon). Connecting alternates → triangle.

**4-fold**: Perpendicular bisector of diameter → 4 cardinal points (90° apart).

**8-fold**: Arcs from 4 cardinal points → 8 divisions (45° apart).

**12-fold**: Combine 4-fold + 6-fold points → 12 divisions (30° apart).

**5-fold / 10-fold** (most demanding): Find midpoint of radius, use it to construct pentagon side length, step 5 times around circumference. Angles: 72° (5-fold), 36° (10-fold). "Requires very careful work to give the compass setting which will exactly meet the starting point."

### Star Formation
- **6-pointed star**: Two overlapping equilateral triangles (Seal of Solomon)
- **8-pointed star (Khatam)**: Two squares rotated 45°
- **10-pointed star (Decagram)**: Connect every 3rd point of decagon
- **12-pointed star**: Three squares at 30° rotations, or connect every 5th of dodecagon

## XIII. SYMMETRY GRID SYSTEMS

### The Three Proportion Families
| System | Key Ratio | Angles | Regional Preference |
|--------|-----------|--------|-------------------|
| 4-fold / 8-fold | √2 (≈1.414) | 90°, 45° | Maghreb, Andalusia |
| 6-fold / 12-fold | √3 (≈1.732) | 60°, 30° | Ubiquitous |
| 5-fold / 10-fold | φ (≈1.618) | 72°, 36° | Iran |

### 4-Fold / 8-Fold (Square Grid)
- √2 governs all relationships: "arcs, lines and rectangles are all related by √2"
- Key tiling: {8,8,4} — alternating octagons and squares, all sides equal
- The **Khatam** (خاتم, 8-pointed star/octagram): "tiles derived from khatam form a jigsaw generating a myriad of designs"
- Self-similar: √2 ratios remain consistent at all scales (fractal-like)
- 4 mirror lines at 45° to each other

### 5-Fold / 10-Fold (Pentagonal)
- Pentagon CANNOT tile periodically — requires girih tiles or quasi-periodic arrangements
- Golden ratio φ = (1+√5)/2: cos(36°) = φ/2, cos(72°) = (φ−1)/2
- All angles multiples of 36° (π/5 radians)
- Can achieve quasi-crystalline (aperiodic) patterns → Penrose connection

### 6-Fold / 12-Fold (Hexagonal Grid)
- Six "mother circles" of equal radius on circumference → rosette dividing into 6 parts
- √3 relationships between hexagon sizes
- Every angle in isometric grid = 60°; every point equidistant from 6 neighbors
- 12-fold: interior angle 150°; dodecagons combine naturally with octagons and hexagons
- 12-fold can generate all 17 wallpaper groups (Hasba method)

## XIV. ROSETTE CONSTRUCTION

### The PIC Method (Polygons-In-Contact)
Formalized from **E.H. Hankin** (1925), refined by A.J. Lee and Craig Kaplan:
1. Position regular polygons edge-to-edge
2. At each edge midpoint, construct two rays at ±θ (contact angle) relative to edge
3. Rays extend until they meet other rays → motif outlines
4. Erase construction polygons

**Contact angle θ** — single parameter controlling star morphology:
- Acute: θ = 36°, Median: θ = 72°, Obtuse: θ = 108°

### Proportioning
Three concentric circles: [oa] outer boundary, [ob] inner star polygon, [oc] petal extensions. Critical circle [P1a] determines ideal proportions.

### Petal Types
- **Parallel**: Spanning 8 divisions; sides parallel
- **Tapered**: Spanning 6 divisions; taper toward tips → {10/3} star

### Viable Rosette Combinations
(12,3), (8,4), (6,6), (5,10) — all four appear in historical patterns.

### The Minimal Triangle
Entire pattern reducible to ONE triangle (8-fold) or TWO (10/12-fold) containing all structural information.

## XV. GIRIH TILES

### The Five Tile Shapes
All edges equal length. All angles multiples of 36°.

| Tile | Persian Name | Sides | Angles |
|------|-------------|-------|--------|
| Decagon | Tabl | 10 | 10 × 144° |
| Pentagon | Pange | 5 | 5 × 108° |
| Bowtie | Sormeh Dan | 6 | 72°, 72°, 216°, 72°, 72°, 216° |
| Hexagon | Shesh Band | 6 | 72°, 144°, 144°, 72°, 144°, 144° |
| Rhombus | Torange | 4 | 72°, 108°, 72°, 108° |

### Girih Line Rules
- Lines cross every edge at **midpoint** at **54°** to edge
- Two lines per edge midpoint
- Adjacent tiles' lines align perfectly → continuous strapwork
- Only girih lines visible in finished work — tile boundaries erased
- Decagon has TWO valid internal patterns (10-fold or 5-fold symmetry)

## XVI. THE PENROSE CONNECTION

### Lu & Steinhardt (Science 315:1106, 2007)
"Decagonal and Quasi-crystalline Tilings in Medieval Islamic Architecture" proved that by ~1200 CE, girih patterns were reconceived as modular tile tessellations enabling quasi-crystalline aperiodic patterns **500 years before Penrose**.

### Darb-i Imam Shrine, Isfahan (1453 CE)
- 231 girih tiles in spandrel
- Approximates Penrose tiling with **11 mismatches in 3,700 units**
- 10-fold rotational symmetry — incompatible with periodicity
- Pattern at **TWO simultaneous scales** — self-similar subdivision
- Large tiles subdivide into smaller girih tiles; scaling factor = **φ** (golden ratio)

### Girih ↔ Penrose Mapping
Five girih shapes encode same information as Penrose P3 tiles (thick/thin rhombi). Subdivision/inflation can repeat infinitely → true aperiodic tiling.

### Historical Timeline
- ~1200 CE: Girih tile approach emerges
- 1197: Gunbad-i Kabud, Maragha — early girih patterns
- 1453: Darb-i Imam — near-perfect quasi-crystal
- 15th–16th c.: Topkapi Scroll documents system
- 1973–74: Penrose discovers aperiodic tilings
- 1984: Shechtman discovers physical quasicrystals
- 2007: Lu & Steinhardt publish proof

## XVII. MUQARNAS (3D Geometric Vaulting)

### Definition
Tiered niche-like elements stacked in corbelled layers, creating honeycomb/stalactite vaults. Persian: **ahoopay**. Transitions square base → circular dome.

### Al-Kashi's Mathematics (Miftah al-Hisab, 1427)
Facets intersect at: 90°, 45°, 135°, or combinations. **Cell** = two facets + roof. **Tier** = adjacent cells on same horizontal plane.

### 2D-to-3D Projection
1. Every muqarnas begins as a 2D plan drawing (toomar)
2. Concentric rings in plan → tiers in 3D
3. Ring subdivisions: squares, rhombi, almonds, bipeds, deltoids, jug shapes, barley kernels
4. Each 2D shape maps to specific 3D curved element
5. No overlap → clean projection possible

### Cell Types (Al-Kashi)
Square, half-square, rhombus, half-rhombus, almond (badam), biped (large/small), jug (jag), barley kernel, deltoid. Large biped and jug derive from square; small biped and almond from rhombus.

### N-gon Tile System (Modern)
32-gon → **16 unique tile types**. Each = rhombus in plan. All edges identical. Tile 1 = 11.25°, Tile 4 = 45°, Tile 8 = 90°, Tile 16 = 180° (flat/vertical "equator"). Seven mirror pairs: 1/15 through 7/9.

### Regional Traditions
| Tradition | Materials | Character |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| North African | Plaster, wood | Freeform, inventive, extreme complexity |
| Iranian | Brick + tile/mirror | Systematic, rule-based, polychrome |
| Ottoman | Stone, marble | Portals/niches, diminished Classical role |
| Egyptian/Syrian | Large stone | Wall-integrated, earthquake-resistant |

### Key Examples
- **Hall of the Two Sisters, Alhambra**: 5,000+ cells, 8m dome, 16 sub-domes, 8-pointed star
- **Ali Qapu, Isfahan**: Cutout muqarnas as **Helmholtz cavity absorbers** — acoustic function
- **Shah Mosque, Isfahan**: 27m entrance iwan, polychrome muqarnas

### The Structural Paradox
"The more ornate a muqarnas is, the more weight it can hold" — each tier increases surface area and structural capacity. Decoration = engineering.

## XVIII. THE 17 WALLPAPER GROUPS

### Exactly 17 two-dimensional symmetry groups exist (Fedorov 1891, Polya 1924)
Crystallographic Restriction Theorem: rotation orders limited to 2, 3, 4, 6 only.

**Parallelogram**: p1 (translations), p2 (180°)
**Rhombus**: cm (reflection + glide), cmm (two reflections + 2-fold)
**Rectangular**: pm, pg, pmm, pmg, pgg
**Square**: p4 (90°), p4m (90° + reflections), p4g (90° + glides)
**Hexagonal**: p3 (120°), p3m1, p31m, p6 (60°), p6m (60° + reflections — most symmetric)

### The Alhambra Debate
Claim that all 17 appear is **contested**. Muller (1944): 11–12 groups. Coxeter found 2 more. Grunbaum (2006): "just two mosaics could yield all 17" if color considered. Most common in Islamic art: **p6m** (sixfold + reflections).

## XIX. HISTORICAL SITES

### Alhambra, Granada (Nasrid, 1238–1492)
- **Alicatado/zellij**: hand-cut glazed ceramic mosaic in black, blue, honey, green, white + luxury golden luster tile
- **Pajarita nazari** (Nasrid bird): signature three-pointed curved star
- Regular **nonagons** (non-constructible with ruler/compass!) — artisans exceeded formal theory
- Key rooms: Hall of Ambassadors ("bones" pattern), Hall of Kings (12-pointed stars), Court of Lions

### The Topkapi Scroll
- 33cm × 29.5m, late 15th/early 16th c., Timurid-Turkmen
- 114 patterns: 59 muqarnas, 16 calligraphic, 44 geometric
- Contains the 5 girih tile outlines + self-similarity subdivision rules
- 9-, 11-, 13-, 16-pointed star figures
- Definitive study: Gulru Necipoglu (Getty, 1995)

### Isfahan, Iran (Safavid)
- **Sheikh Lotfollah Mosque**: The Peacock Dome — ogival compartments diminishing toward apex; peacock without tail (sun creates tail through lattice windows!)
- **Shah Mosque**: Haft-rang (seven-color) tiles, intentional "symmetrical asymmetries"
- **Darb-i Imam**: Quasi-crystalline pattern at two simultaneous scales
- Persian: 10-fold symmetry, colorful dome exteriors, golden/turquoise on dark blue

### Samarkand, Uzbekistan (Timurid)
- **Banna'i**: glazed tiles alternated with plain bricks
- **Square Kufic**: Arabic as rectilinear brickwork
- Shah-i-Zinda: all Timurid decorative types in one complex
- Tuman Aqa Mausoleum (1405): advanced 10/3 decagonal pattern

### Regional Signatures
| Region | Signature Technique | Geometry Emphasis |
|--------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Moorish/Andalusian | Zellij/alicatado | Pure geometry, possibly all 17 groups |
| Mamluk (Egypt/Syria) | Carved stone domes | 6-, 8-, 12-point stars |
| Persian/Iranian | Girih, haft-rang | 10-fold, quasi-crystalline, φ |
| Timurid/Central Asian | Banna'i, Square Kufic | Monumental, "Timurid blue" |
| Ottoman | Iznik tiles | Floral over geometric, Rumi-Hatayi |
| Mughal (India) | Jali screens, parchin kari | Solid/void, naturalistic inlay |

## XX. MODERN TOOLS & SCHOLARS

### Craig S. Kaplan (University of Waterloo)
PhD: "Computer Graphics and Geometric Ornamental Design" (2002). Two methods: **Najm** (user-selected motifs + inference) and **Hankin** (single contact angle θ parameter). Papers: "Computer Generated Islamic Star Patterns" (2000), "Islamic Star Patterns in Absolute Geometry" with Salesin (ACM TOG 2004), "Polygons in Contact" (2005), "Freeform Islamic Geometric Patterns" with Lin (2023).

### Eric Broug
Author: *Islamic Geometric Patterns* and *Islamic Geometric Design* (Thames & Hudson). Compass-and-straightedge only approach. Three families: square, hexagon, pentagon. School of Islamic Geometric Design. TED-Ed lesson.

### Jay Bonner
*Islamic Geometric Patterns* (Springer, 2017, 595pp). Definitive modern reference. Classification: 5 historical systems — regular polygon, two fourfold, fivefold, sevenfold.

### The Yazdi Bandi Method (یزدی بندی)
Iranian tradition for dome interior patterns. Key unit: **Sine Fakhri** (سینه فخری). Evolved from Rasmi-bandi through geometric fragmentation. Il-Khanid dynasty (13th c.). The **Karbandi** system: masonry lattice space dome, polygon inscribed in base circle, connection distance determines rib geometry. Moroccan equivalent: **Hasba** (حسبة) method.
