### Lighting Journey
- Started with directional + rect area + ambient + fill
- **Hard shadow artifacts** from directional light were a problem
- Tawfeeq's idea: replace directional with large RectAreaLight — no shadows = no artifacts
- Final setup: ALL rect-area based (no shadow map needed)
  - **Key**: RectAreaLight warm `0xfff5e8`, intensity 3.5, 250×250, at (-50, 140, 250)
  - **Cool fill**: RectAreaLight `0xc8d8f0`, intensity 1.2, 200×200, from right (120, 40, 150)
  - **Spot accent**: SpotLight warm on lower dial area
  - **Point spec**: PointLight intensity 15 at (30, 60, 180) for tight highlights
  - **Ambient**: 0.3
  - Shadows DISABLED entirely (`renderer.shadowMap.enabled = false`)
- ACES filmic tone mapping at exposure 1.5
- HDRI tried extensively (Polyhaven studio_small_03, _08, _09, loft_hall) — caused async blowout on load. Removed.
- Procedural env map also tried — didn't work well with RectAreaLights in env scene
- Key lesson: **high metalness + no env map = dark hands**. Solved by dropping metalness to 0.5 with high clearcoat

### Key Lessons
- RectAreaLight doesn't cast shadows — feature, not bug, for clean look
- ACES tone mapping crushes dark tones — need higher exposure for dark dials
- PBR metals NEED env maps for reflections — without one, lower metalness + clearcoat is the workaround
- External HDRIs load async → visual pop on load. Procedural envs or no env = instant stable render
- `envMapIntensity = 0` per-material prevents HDRI from washing non-metal surfaces
