### What Needs to Happen Next
Tawfeeq's core request: glass cube that:
1. Reads LIGHTER than the 18% grey floor

3. Has the spectral dispersion rays and floor caustic colors
4. Camera: wide shot (4.6, 6.8, 7.2 / FOV 78°)

### Technical Options for Next Attempt
**Option A**: MeshPhysicalMaterial but with a proper HDRI environment (not RoomEnvironment).
  - Use a bright studio HDRI to drive reflections. Without HDRI, the env has very little to reflect.
  - But HDRIs need to load from URL which may be slow/unreliable.

**Option B**: FBO technique but with a BRIGHT background scene (background 0x606070+, strong backdrop plane lit at > 50% brightness). The glass refracts bright content → looks bright.
  - Key: turn up the backdrop color to ~0x909090 and make the backlight VERY strong on it.

**Option C**: Hybrid — MeshPhysicalMaterial body + FBO overlay just for the chromatic dispersion tint on faces.

**Option D**: Fake it with an additive inner-glow mesh — just add a semi-transparent, slightly emissive inner cube that makes the glass body BRIGHT regardless of background. Standard product photography glass trick: add internal light source.

**Most promising**: Option D or B. The issue has been that we're trying to be physically correct but the dark scene keeps killing the glass. A fake inner glow (emissive/additive mesh inside the cube) would guarantee the cube reads brighter than the floor regardless of the background.
