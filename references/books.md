# Reference Books — Islamic Floral Illustration

## To Buy (physical only, no digital editions)

### 1. Motifs in Turkish Decorative Arts
- **Authors:** Dr. İnci A. Birol & Prof. Dr. Çiçek Derman
- **Publisher:** Kubbealtı Neşriyatı, Istanbul (1991, 22nd ed. 2025)
- **ISBN:** 9789757663072
- **Pages:** 206 + 16 color plates, b/w illustrations
- **Language:** Turkish + English translation
- **Why:** THE authoritative reference for hatâyî, penç, goncagül, leaf motifs. Construction drawings with proportional rules. Directly translates to procedural code.
- **Buy:**
  - AbeBooks (~$35): https://www.abebooks.com/Motifs-Turkish-decorative-arts-Turk-tezyini/11826247016/bd
  - Amazon: https://www.amazon.com/Turk-tezyini-sanatlarinda-motifler-decorative/dp/B01FIZIXLO

### 2. Islamic Art of Illumination: Classical Tazhib from Ottoman to Contemporary Times
- **Author:** Sema Onat
- **ISBN:** 9781935295822
- **Why:** Full plates of roses, buds, stylized flowers with gold. Shows three-shade layering technique for petals. The exact motif vocabulary we need.
- **Buy:**

## Key Design Vocabulary (from research)

- **Penç** — flower viewed from TOP (bird's eye). Radial symmetry, typically 5-fold. Three concentric rings of petals. Our small fill flowers.
- **Hatâyî** — flower viewed from SIDE (vertical section). Shows petal layering and depth. Our landmark roses.
- **Goncagül** — rosebud in profile. Tight, wrapped petals. Our buds.
- **Three-shade technique** — each petal gets 3 tones of same color (light base → medium → dark edge).
- **Tahrir** — fine black or colored contour lines defining each petal.
- **Rüstem Pasha penç structure** — three concentric rings: two outer rings of 12 double-lobed petals, central curvilinear pattern of 12 petals.
